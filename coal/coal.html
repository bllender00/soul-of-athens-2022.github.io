THE LITTLE CITIES

The Little Cities Coal is widespread in Southeast Ohio. Mined and unmined, the history of coal is vast.Before humans began the coal industry that was integral in settling the Little Cities of Black Diamonds glaciers roamed. They pushed sediment out of the way to flatten out much of Ohio, but at the edge of what we know as the Appalachian foothills, they stopped. Nowhere is this more apparent than in Perry County, the number one producer of coal in Ohio in the late 1870s and early 1880s, according to Chris Wilson. Wilson, who grew up in Shawnee, Ohio, currently works at the Little Cities of Black Diamonds council (LCBD). The LCBD is an organization dedicated to teaching and preserving knowledge about the cities developed as mining towns in Southeast Ohio
